Office Plant Care

Šibenik open office · NASA plants · v2 tracking

Goal: all plants either checked or watered on each working day
Sat/Sun: “no one in the office” by default
Knowledge base
General playbook schedule, roles, climate

Owner: one person (plus backup) runs checks. Weekly in warm months, every 2 weeks in cool months.[web:32]

Decision rule: check soil 3–4 cm deep; if dry for that plant’s profile, water. If still moist, mark “checked, skip”.[web:33][web:43]

Climate: hot, bright summers; mild winters. Move plants back from glass in July–August and closer in winter.[web:34][web:37][web:44]

Weekends: treated as “no office presence” but you can still log emergency waterings.

Care profiles who drinks how often

Thirsty (3–7 days summer, 7–14 winter): bamboo palm, peace lily, Chinese evergreen. Keep soil lightly moist, never soggy.[web:34][web:40]

Moderate (7–10 days summer, 14–21 winter): spider plant, pothos, dracaena. Let top soil dry between waterings.[web:34][web:40]

Drought tolerant (10–21+ days): snake plant, sometimes ivy. Let most of the root zone dry before watering.[web:34][web:40][web:43]

Light: bright indirect near windows for palms/peace lily; medium for spider, pothos, dracaena; low–medium for snake plant and aglaonema.[web:34][web:44]

Maintenance beyond water pests, cleaning, rotation
  • Quick pest scan on each round (webbing, sticky leaves, cottony blobs).[web:32][web:38]
  • Leaf wipe monthly for big leaves to remove dust and improve gas exchange.[web:34][web:43]
  • Fertilize lightly during growing season, about monthly; pause in winter.[web:34][web:40]
  • Rotate pots a quarter turn monthly to avoid leaning toward light.[web:32][web:41]
